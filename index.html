Magellan's Cross

Magellan’s Cross is a historical site in Cebu where Ferdinand Magellan planted a cross in 1521. It symbolizes the arrival of Christianity in the Philippines. Visitors can see the original cross housed in a small chapel.

Basilica Del Sto. Nino

The Basilica del Sto. Niño is a famous church in Cebu, home to the oldest religious image in the Philippines. It was built to honor the Santo Niño, a symbol of faith and devotion. People come to pray and experience the church's rich history and beauty.

Oslob Cebu

Oslob is a coastal town in Cebu known for its whale shark watching. Visitors can swim with these gentle giants in crystal-clear waters. It’s a popular spot for an unforgettable and unique ocean adventure.

Simala Shrine

The Simala Shrine is a beautiful church in Sibonga, Cebu, known for its miraculous image of the Virgin Mary. It’s a peaceful place where people come to pray and seek blessings. The stunning architecture and serene surroundings make it a popular pilgrimage site.

Temple of Leah

The Temple of Leah is a grand, Roman-inspired temple in Cebu, built as a tribute to a wife’s love and devotion. It features impressive statues, beautiful gardens, and breathtaking views of the city. It’s a must-visit for those seeking both history and scenic beauty.

Fort San Pedro

Fort San Pedro is a historic military defense structure in Cebu, built by the Spanish in the 16th century. It offers a glimpse into the past with its well-preserved walls and exhibits. Visitors can explore its rich history and enjoy views of the sea and surrounding areas.
